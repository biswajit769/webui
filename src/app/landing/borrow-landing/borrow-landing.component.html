What methods can I use to borrow money through RTM?

At RTM, we realized that real-world, practicable lending solutions are the need of the day. Through extensive field tests and focus group sessions with borrowers in Karnataka, we discovered that the current crop of alternative lending platforms do not offer flexible borrowing as part of their gamut of services. As a result, customers using these platforms were being tied down with rigid loan agreements that did not adapt to their requirements. We saw this as an opportunity waiting to be addressed. How? We devised a few scenarios which most closely represent everyday borrowing. But before we dive into each scenario, we want you to understand the concept of a Credit Limit on RTM.

A Credit Limit is assigned to each borrower who registers on RTM. It is a threshold value that indicates the total amount you are eligible to borrow via RTM. The Credit Limit is derived from the financial information we collect from you in your account statements, salary slips, etc. Think of the Credit Limit as a ceiling for your capacity to borrow on RTM. The sum of your ongoing loans in RTM and any new loans you are requesting on RTM must not exceed the Credit Limit. The following example illustrates this concept:

Let us suppose that you are assigned a Credit Limit of ₹ 2,00,000 on RTM. You have ongoing loans on RTM which total ₹ 1,00,000. Lets call this amount A. You are requesting for a new loan on RTM for ₹ 50,000. Let us call this amount B. Rule: A + B ≤ Credit Limit In this case, 1,00,000 + 50,000 ≤ 2,00,000. As this is valid, your request for the new loan of ₹ 50,000 is also valid, and you can proceed to request for this loan on RTM.

The Credit Limit is closely linked to the scenarios we have developed. Read on below to understand how each scenario makes borrowing simpler, faster and adaptable to your everyday needs.

Scenario One: Silver Plan

When you register as a borrower and join our growing family of borrowers on RTM, you become a Silver Plan member of the marketplace. The Silver Plan enables you to borrow money from RTM's NBFC as long as the total of the funds you request for as well as funds from your ongoing loans fall within your Credit Limit.

This constitutes Scenario One.

Scenario Two: Gold Plan

Now let’s say a borrower wants to apply for a loan. Unfortunately, this loan exceeds the borrower’s Credit Limit, and he is unable to place his request for this loan on the marketplace. What can the borrower do now?

In this situation, the borrower can get in touch with a NBFC known to him/her and provide the NBFC with his/her unique Virtual ID. The Virtual ID is powerful tool for borrowers to identify and interact with RTM's NBFC. Using the Virtual ID, the NBFC can look up the borrower in on RTM and can upgrade the borrower to the Gold Plan, thereby increasing the borrower’s Credit Limit. Now the RTM's NBFC will be able to successfully lend money to the same borrower.

This constitutes Scenario Two. class="bults"
